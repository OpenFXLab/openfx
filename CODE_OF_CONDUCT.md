# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

OpenFXLab is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and professional environment for everyone who participates in this project — contributors, users, and community members of all backgrounds and experience levels.

---

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and professional in all interactions.
- Provide and receive feedback constructively. Critique ideas, not people.
- Acknowledge and welcome different perspectives and experiences.
- Ask questions openly. There are no foolish questions in a technical project.
- Give credit where it is due.
- Respect the scope and direction of the project, even when disagreeing with specific decisions.

---

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Personal attacks or insults, whether public or private.
- Deliberately dismissive or condescending behavior.
- Publishing private information about any person without consent.
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project, or off-topic disruption.
- Any behavior that would reasonably make someone feel unwelcome.

---

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, email communications, and any other forum associated with OpenFXLab.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to:

**[openfxresearch@gmail.com](mailto:openfxresearch@gmail.com)**

Reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. The project lead will review reports and respond appropriately.

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this code of conduct may result in warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent removal from the project, depending on the severity and circumstances.

---

## Attribution

This code of conduct is written specifically for OpenFXLab and reflects the values of a small, professional open-source collaboration.
